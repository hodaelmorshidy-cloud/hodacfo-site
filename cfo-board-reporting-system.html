Common Questions

Does the PowerPoint update automatically from the Excel?

No — and that’s deliberate. Live Excel-to-PowerPoint links break across versions and machines and embarrass you at the worst moment. The deck is a pre-formatted template filled with the same worked example; you replace the figures with your own. The Start Here tab lists exactly which cells map to which slide. A clean manual update beats a broken link in front of a board.

Does it work in Google Sheets?

No. This workbook is built for Microsoft Excel and uses Excel-native features. It is not compatible with Google Sheets. Requires Excel 2019, 2021, or Microsoft 365 (Windows or Mac).

Do I need macros or VBA?

No — zero macros. Everything runs on standard Excel formulas. No add-ins, no Power Query, no structured tables that break on row insert. Built for backward compatibility with Excel 2019.

Will my numbers reconcile like the sample’s?

If your inputs are internally consistent, yes. The balance check, cash-flow reconciliation, and retained-earnings bridge are live — they confirm with a green ✓ or flag the exact dollar gap if something doesn’t tie.

What does IFRS 18 transition-ready mean?

IFRS 18 is effective for annual periods beginning on or after 1 January 2027, superseding IAS 1. It introduces two new required P&L subtotals (operating profit; profit before financing and income taxes) and management-defined performance measure (MPM) disclosures. The Commentary tab includes a dedicated board-ready IFRS 18 transition note covering all three requirements.

Can I change the fiscal year, currency, or company name?

Yes — all controlled from the Settings tab. Change company name, fiscal year, currency, reporting month, comparative period, headcount, and DSO/DPO targets in one place. The rest of the workbook and report headers update automatically.
