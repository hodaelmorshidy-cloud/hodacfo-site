What is a reverse DCF?

A reverse DCF works backwards: instead of projecting cash flows to find fair value, it asks “what growth rate does the current stock price already assume?” If the implied growth rate is higher than what you believe is realistic, the stock may be overvalued. The Reverse DCF tab solves for the implied 5-year revenue CAGR at any market price. If implied CAGR exceeds 20%, the stock may be pricing near-perfection from an already-stretched valuation.

What is margin of safety?

Margin of safety is the discount to DCF fair value at which you would buy a stock. A 20% margin of safety on a $95 fair value means you only buy at $76 or below — protecting you against model error and unexpected negative events. The Margin of Safety tab calculates your entry price at 10%, 20%, and 30% discounts, and also solves for the maximum purchase price at a target IRR.
